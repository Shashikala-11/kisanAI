WHEAT (Triticum aestivum) — Punjab
=====================================
Sowing window: November 1–25 (optimal Nov 1–15 for maximum yield)
Late sowing (after Nov 25): yield drops 35–40 kg/hectare per day of delay

Recommended varieties 2024-25:
- HD-3086: High yield (55 q/ha), rust resistant, 155 days maturity
- PBW-725: Good for late sowing, 148 days, tolerates heat stress
- DBW-187: Premium quality, 157 days, suitable for all Punjab zones
- WH-1270: Drought tolerant, good for Malwa region
- PBW-826: New release, high protein content, 152 days

Fertilizer (per hectare):
- N: 120 kg, P2O5: 60 kg, K2O: 40 kg
- Apply: 1/3 N + full P + full K at sowing (basal)
- 1/3 N at first irrigation (21 DAS — crown root initiation stage)
- 1/3 N at second irrigation (42 DAS — tillering stage)
- Zinc sulphate 25 kg/ha if soil Zn < 0.6 ppm (common in Punjab)
- Use neem-coated urea to reduce N loss by 10-15%

Irrigation schedule (5-6 irrigations critical):
1. Crown Root Initiation (CRI): 20-21 DAS — MOST CRITICAL, never skip
2. Tillering: 40-45 DAS
3. Jointing: 60-65 DAS
4. Flowering: 80-85 DAS
5. Grain filling: 100-105 DAS
6. Dough stage: 115-120 DAS (skip if rain)

Common diseases:
- Yellow rust (Puccinia striiformis): Yellow stripes on leaves. Spray Propiconazole 25% EC 1ml/L
- Brown rust: Orange pustules. Spray Mancozeb 75% WP 2g/L
- Karnal bunt: Black powder in grains. Use certified seed, seed treatment with Carboxin 75% WP
- Loose smut: Black spores replacing grain. Seed treatment with Tebuconazole 2% DS

Harvest: April 15 – May 10. Moisture should be 12-14% at harvest.
Expected yield: 50-55 q/ha under good management, 40-45 q/ha average

MSP 2024-25: ₹2,275/quintal (Government of India)
